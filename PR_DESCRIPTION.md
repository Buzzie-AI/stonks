## Investment Thesis

Figma represents a compelling opportunity to average down on an existing position while investing in a high-growth SaaS company with strong fundamentals. Despite the current portfolio position being down 45.97% from entry, Figma's Q4 2025 results demonstrate accelerating revenue growth (40% YoY), improving profitability (14% non-GAAP operating margin in Q4), and exceptional customer retention (136% Net Dollar Retention Rate). The stock is trading at a significant discount from its 52-week high of $142.92, presenting an attractive entry point for a company that just crossed $1B in annual revenue and is guiding for 30% growth in 2026.

## Fundamental Analysis

**Revenue & Growth Metrics:**
- Full-year 2025 revenue: $1.056 billion, up 41% year-over-year
- Q4 2025 revenue: $303.8 million, up 40% year-over-year (accelerating from prior quarters)
- International revenue grew 45% year-over-year, showing strong global expansion
- 2026 guidance: $1.366-$1.374 billion (30% growth), demonstrating continued strong momentum

**Profitability:**
- Non-GAAP net income: $166.8 million for full year 2025
- Non-GAAP operating margin: 12% for full year, improving to 14% in Q4 2025
- Operating cash flow margin: 24% for full year, indicating strong cash generation
- $1.7 billion in cash and marketable securities, providing financial flexibility

**Customer Metrics (Key SaaS Indicators):**
- Net Dollar Retention Rate: 136% (up from 131% prior quarter), indicating strong expansion within existing customer base
- 13,861 customers generating $10,000+ ARR (up significantly year-over-year)
- 1,405 customers generating $100,000+ ARR, showing enterprise traction

**Market Position:**
- Dominant player in collaborative design tools market
- Strong competitive moat through network effects (designers collaborate within Figma)
- Adobe acquisition termination (2022 deal) means Figma remains independent and focused on growth

## Technical Analysis

- Trading at significant discount to growth trajectory and fundamentals
- Current market cap: ~$15 billion, which appears undervalued relative to $1B+ revenue run rate and 30%+ growth

## Risk Factors

- **Existing Position Risk**: Portfolio already holds 100 shares at $48.18 average cost, currently down 45.97%. Adding to a losing position requires conviction that fundamentals justify averaging down rather than cutting losses.

- **Valuation Compression**: Despite strong fundamentals, SaaS multiples have compressed across the sector. Further multiple compression could limit upside even with strong execution.

- **Competition**: Adobe (with XD), Canva, and other design tools continue to compete. While Figma has strong network effects, competitive pressure could impact growth rates.

- **Operating Margin Guidance**: Management expects non-GAAP operating margin to decline to 8% in 2026 (from 12% in 2025) due to AI infrastructure investments and hybrid monetization model transition. This could pressure profitability in the near term.

- **Macro Sensitivity**: Enterprise software spending could slow in an economic downturn, impacting Figma's growth trajectory despite strong current metrics.

- **Concentration Risk**: Adding to FIG increases portfolio concentration in a single position that is already significantly underwater.

## Time Horizon & Confidence

- **Time Horizon:** Medium-term (6-12 months) — Expecting the stock to recover as the company continues executing on strong fundamentals and the market recognizes the value disconnect between current price and growth trajectory.

- **Confidence:** Medium — Strong fundamental metrics and growth trajectory support the thesis, but the existing underwater position and sector headwinds create uncertainty. The 136% NDR and accelerating revenue growth provide conviction, but execution on margin guidance and competitive dynamics remain key variables.
